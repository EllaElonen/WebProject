Vector images

Converting a bitmapped image into svg

There are many ways of adding an svg image to your site. To begin with, you could convert a bitmapped image to svg. Certain image editing programs allow you to export a bitmaped image as a vector image. Inkscape was used to convert the following image to svg format. You may want to have a bitmapped image as a backup for browsers that do not support svg, like in this example. 

                <object data="images/Dreaming.svg" width="300" height="100" type="image/svg+xml">
                    <img src="images/Dreaming.jpg" alt="Dreaming"/>
                <object>

Drawing an svg image in HTML

You can also draw your own svg image. For example:

The example above illustrates how you can draw shapes in your svg drawing. Other options besides circle are rectangle, ellipse, line, polyline, polygon and path. You can fill the shapes with color, and stroke the lines and curves with color.

The following is another example that draws a rectangle with rounded corners, and also illustrates how you can fill the shape with opacity, which makes it more transparent. The color is the same in both of these figures. Here, the width and height is also increased. 

                <svg width="400" height="180">
                    <rect x="50" y="20" rx="20" ry="20" width="150" height="150" fill="blue" stroke="pink" stroke-width="4" fill-opacity="0.1" fill-opacity="0.9"/>
                </svg>

The path tag can be used for drawing different kinds of lines and curves. This is an example of two staight lines and a bezier curve filled with blue.
